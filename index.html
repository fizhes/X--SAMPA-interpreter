SAMPA (Speech Assessment Methods Phonetic Alphabet) is a phonetic transcription system based on the IPA (Internation Phonetic Alphabet).

Non-English letters of the IPA are encoded in SAMPA using capital letters (A → ɑ, for instance), other ASCII characters ({ → æ, for instance), and characters followed by backslashes (j\ → ʝ, for instance).

Certain diacritics (such as _d → ̪ ) and suprasegmental symbols (such as " → ˈ) are encoded as well.

Several SAMPA alphabets were created for different languages. X-SAMPA was created to unify them and include every IPA symbol.

This system, X*-SAMPA, includes every X-SAMPA encoding, along with some additional encodings for implicit or obselete IPA symbols and common non-IPA phonetic symbols.


Additional notes:

The implicit IPA symbol for the retroflex click is the alveolar click symbol ǃ with the retroflex hook. That symbol is supported in Unicode, but doesn't render in most fonts. It is for this reason that the symbol is not encoded, and it is recommended to use the common but unofficial symbol ‼ (!\`).

In addition to those listed above, a large portion of the IPA has superscript variants supported in Unicode. Those that are also supported by most fonts are encoded as the encoding for the standard symbol, preceded by +. For instance, ɲ is encoded as J and n\, so the superscript ᶮ is encoded as +J and +n\.
